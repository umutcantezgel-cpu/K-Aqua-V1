The construction sector is responsible for a significant portion of global CO2 emissions. Consequently, pressure is growing on architects and planners to use more sustainable and lower-emission materials. In building services engineering (MEP), the piping systems used play an often underestimated but significant role in a building's life cycle assessment.

## The Carbon Footprint: Plastic vs. Metal

Many planners instinctively assume that metallic pipes (like copper or steel) are more "natural" and therefore more environmentally friendly than plastics. However, a holistic Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) paints a completely different picture.

**PP-RCT beats metal in 3 key sustainability categories:**

1. **Manufacturing & Energy Consumption**
   Extracting and processing ores into copper or steel pipes requires enormous amounts of energy and generates immense CO2 emissions (through blast furnaces, melting processes, etc.). Polypropylene, on the other hand, is processed at significantly lower temperatures, drastically reducing energy requirements during production.
   Furthermore, K-Aqua utilizes a closed cooling water circuit in the production process, which completely eliminates the potential pollution of rivers, streams, and lakes.

   > **Did you know?** Before polypropylene was invented, gaseous oil wastes such as propylene and ethylene were simply burned off as they were considered useless. Today, exactly these gases are used to manufacture high-quality polypropylene, which massively reduces air pollution caused by flaring.
   
2. **Transport Weight**
   A plastic pipe made of PP-RCT weighs only a fraction of a comparable steel pipe. When supplying a high-rise project with pipes, you require significantly fewer truck trips for plastic systems. This enormously reduces transport emissions. Try our [CO2 Calculator](/en/co2-rechner) to compute the exact transport savings for your project!
   
3. **Lifespan and Recycling**
   While metal pipes often have to be replaced after 15-20 years due to corrosion (which means renewed material expenditure), K-Aqua PP-RCT systems remain maintenance-free for over 50 years. At the end of its life cycle, polypropylene is **100% recyclable** (Recycling Code "5") and can be efficiently sorted and reused for other plastic products. At K-Aqua, we also consistently return all production waste and offcuts to the recycling loop.

## Benefits for Green Building Certificates

The use of demonstrably sustainable piping systems contributes directly to the point catalogs of certifications such as **LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design)** or **BREEAM**. 

In addition, the excellent thermal insulation capacity of PP-RCT (especially with our [multilayer composite pipes](/en/produkte/pipes)) helps to minimize energy losses during the circulation of hot water or cooling media. This improves the overall energy efficiency of the building.
